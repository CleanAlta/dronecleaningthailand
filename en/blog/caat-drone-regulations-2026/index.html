CAAT Drone Regulations 2026: Complete Guide for Commercial Operations

Updated guide to commercial drone operations compliance in Thailand, including the new UAS Portal system.

Published February 15, 2026 · Updated March 14, 2026 · 6 min read

Thailand's dual-authority drone regulation system

Commercial drone operations in Thailand require registration with two separate agencies: CAAT (Civil Aviation Authority of Thailand) and NBTC (National Broadcasting and Telecommunications Commission).

CAAT registration (airspace authority)

As of September 1, 2025, all drone operators must hold a valid drone aviation certificate issued through the new UAS Portal (uasportal.caat.or.th). The legacy registration system was permanently closed June 30, 2025. Requirements include passing a 40-question online exam with a 75% pass threshold, registering each drone, and submitting flight plans at least 3 days in advance via the UAS Portal.

NBTC registration (radio frequency)

All drones with radio transmitters require NBTC registration regardless of weight or use. Registration costs approximately 214 THB and takes 1–3 business days.

Insurance requirements

Commercial drone operations require minimum THB 1,000,000 third-party liability insurance per flight. CleanAlta maintains THB 2,000,000 coverage. Insurance must be obtained before CAAT registration.

Bangkok restricted zone

The Bangkok VTR1 Restricted Zone covers a 19 km radius from Victory Monument at 3,000 feet — encompassing virtually all of central Bangkok. Most cleaning operations in metropolitan Bangkok require explicit CAAT authorization beyond standard registration.

Key 2025-2026 changes

  • New UAS Portal launched, replacing legacy system
  • Drone aviation certificate now mandatory (exam-based)
  • Drones over 25 kg allowed for civilian use with approval
  • Thailand drafting ~30 new drone measures to align with ICAO standards
  • Drone delivery pilot project launched in Bangkok (June 2025)
